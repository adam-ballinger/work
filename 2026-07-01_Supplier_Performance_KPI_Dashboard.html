Executive Summary

    Fill Rate — Target 98%

    About This Metric

    What it measures: A purchase order (PO) is considered "filled" when all ordered SKU quantities are received at Home Depot's Rapid Deployment Center (RDC). Fill Rate reflects how completely we ship what Home Depot ordered.
    How it's calculated: Quantity Received ÷ Quantity Ordered on the PO. Home Depot aggregates weekly data into a monthly result and evaluates each PO over an 8-week assessment window starting the week it's expected to arrive at the RDC.
    Threshold & offset: Home Depot's expectation is 100%, with a 98% compliance threshold. If our actualized Fill Rate falls below 98%, we're charged a financial offset equal to 10% of the value of each unshipped unit.
    How the performance management program works: Home Depot evaluates performance monthly at the Merchandise Vendor (MVendor) level through its Supplier Performance Management (SPM) tool, then applies any financial offsets at the Payables Vendor level on the next payment cycle. "Pre Dispute" is the raw, as-reported result; when we believe a violation is incorrect, we file a formal Non-Compliance Dispute, and "Post Dispute" reflects the outcome after that review.

    On-Time Delivery — Target 90%

    About This Metric

    What it measures: Since our shipments are prepaid to Home Depot's RDC, this metric is simply whether each shipment arrived on schedule.
    How it's calculated: A shipment is on-time if its Trailer Check-In timestamp at the RDC is on or before the Expected Delivery Date on the PO.
    Threshold & offset: Home Depot's expectation is 100%, with a 90% compliance threshold. Missing it triggers a financial offset equal to 10% of the gross value of each late shipment.
    How the performance management program works: Same monthly cadence as Fill Rate — Home Depot evaluates performance at the Merchandise Vendor (MVendor) level via the SPM tool and applies offsets at the Payables Vendor level. A formal Non-Compliance Dispute Process is available if we believe a late-shipment call is wrong; "Pre Dispute" vs. "Post Dispute" in the chart above reflect that review.
